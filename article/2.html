Key rules and tips to polish your English writing.

Why Grammar Matters

Good grammar ensures your ideas are conveyed clearly and professionally. It prevents miscommunication, strengthens your arguments, and enhances the reader’s trust. Whether writing emails, reports, or articles, mastering the basics is essential.

Parts of Speech

All English words fall into eight categories: nouns name people or things, verbs show action or state, adjectives describe, adverbs modify, pronouns replace nouns, prepositions show relationships, conjunctions connect, and interjections express emotion.

Punctuation Essentials

• Period. Ends statements.
• Comma, separates items and clauses.
• Semicolon; links related independent clauses.
• Colon: introduces lists or explanations.
• Dash— adds emphasis or interruption.
• Quotation “ ” encloses speech or titles.

Common Grammar Pitfalls

  • Subject–verb agreement: “She writes,” not “She write.”
  • Its vs. It’s: Its possessive vs. it’s = “it is.”
  • Their/There/They’re: Possessive vs. location vs. contraction.
  • Run-on sentences: Split or use proper punctuation.

Quick Polishing Tips

1. Read your draft aloud to catch awkward phrasing.
2. Use active voice where possible (“The team achieved…”).
3. Vary sentence length to maintain rhythm.
4. Keep a style guide or checklist handy.
5. Use tools like spellcheckers—but don’t rely solely on them.
